    Reserves — how much to hold per merchant
    The hold percent is built from the band above plus the merchant's other risk signals, recomputed live, and applied to the last-3-month GMV.

    The logic

    Reserve % = base(band) + category + country + mismatch + churn + auto-payout + new-merchant, capped 1-45%, held against last-3-month GMV.
    Base by band (from the Monte Carlo dispute-rate bands above): Elite 1, Best 2, Good 3, Standard 5, Watch 10, Warning 18, Critical 28, Unclassified 6.
    Category: nsfw +6, gambling +6, iptv +5, gaming +4, financial_services +4, ai_business +2, edtech +2, others/astrology +2, digital_products +1.

    Correlation explorer
    How to read this tabAnswers does A move with B for every pairing, in plain words. Pick an outcome (disputes, refunds, failures) and read the ranked list: each line says the strength and a clear Yes, correlated or Not correlated. Correlation is not proof of cause, it is a signal of what tends to travel together.
    Tests how strongly any two things move together, computed live on the current data. It automatically uses the right statistical test for each pair (Spearman for two numbers, the correlation ratio for a number vs a category, Cramer's V for two categories), labels the strength in plain English, and states plainly whether they are correlated. Strength bands: under 0.10 none, 0.10 to 0.30 weak, 0.30 to 0.50 moderate, 0.50 to 0.70 strong, 0.70+ very strong. "Correlated" means statistically significant (p < 0.05) and at least weak.

    Statistical validation

    Run on the current 6,909 active merchants. These results justify the model rather than asserting it.
    1. Reserve weights are regression-derived
    Poisson regression of disputes on all factors at once (offset = log transactions). Each weight = round(6 x ln of the multicollinearity-adjusted rate-ratio), so the numbers come from the data, not judgment.
    FactorAdjusted rate-ratiop-valueWeightNote
    High-risk country1.91x4e-70+4strongest adjustment, highly significant
    Churn / orphan1.57x2e-27+3significant
    New (<150 days)1.16x0.016+1small but significant
    Country mismatch1.02x0.65+1NOT significant after controls; cut from +5 to a minimal flag
    Previous hand-set weights were +8 / +6 / +4 / +5. The model shows they were over-weighted, and that mismatch carries no independent signal once country and category are controlled. Reserve average accordingly moves from 14.9% to 9.7%.
    2. Multicollinearity (VIF): no double-counting
    card share 1.17, wallet share 1.11, failure rate 1.04, AOV 1.02, tenure 1.15, trailing-zeros 1.18 — all far below the 5.0 concern line, so the additive factors are not redundant. (GMV and AOV do correlate at 0.90, so only one of them is ever used.)
    3. Fast-track onboarding: statistically feasible
    The clean-fingerprint segment (no mismatch + data-low-risk country + low-risk category) is 306 merchants with a 0.194% pooled dispute rate (95% CI 0.18% to 0.20%), against the 0.90% network monitoring line. The upper confidence bound is roughly a quarter of the line and only 3.3% ever breach it, so auto-onboarding this segment at weekly payout is safe on the evidence.
    4. Out-of-sample prediction: AUC 0.881
    A model flagging merchants whose dispute rate exceeds the 0.90% line scores 0.881 (plus/minus 0.013) AUC under 5-fold cross-validation, where 0.5 is chance and 1.0 is perfect. Merchant-level prediction is strong once a merchant has transacted; a brand-new merchant with no history is handled by the segment fingerprint in point 3.
    5. Reserve adequacy backtest
    Against actual dispute exposure across the 385 merchants with disputes (about $530k), the model holds ~68% coverage today. The global reserve scale is a single lever that can be raised toward full coverage. True out-of-time backtesting needs month-stamped disputes, which this export does not carry, so cross-validation and exposure coverage stand in for it.

    How this works

    Atlas reads its own source sheet directly as CSV (no Supabase). Every load and the Refresh button fully wipe in-memory data and reload the whole sheet. Fill an amber blank in the sheet, hit Refresh, and every number, band, policy and chart recomputes. The thresholds Monte Carlo runs entirely in your browser. Only active (transacted) merchants are used for rates; the full base is used for counts and onboarding.
